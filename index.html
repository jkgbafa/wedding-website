our story

How it all began

It all started in a church car park. Lucia came by to ask Joshua's friend for a power bank — and from that very first sight, Joshua was smitten. He gladly offered to fetch his own power bank to save Lucia's day.

Their paths kept crossing after that. Joshua kept forgetting her name, and each time Lucia would warmly, graciously remind him — and it was that softness and humility that drew him in. A quiet friendship began, and surely it blossomed into something neither of them could ignore — and now, into marriage.

It would be so special to have you with us. We would be deeply grateful if you'd join us to celebrate our love and see us into this next season together.

Q & A

When is the RSVP deadline?

Please RSVP by 30 September 2026 — it helps us plan seats, food, and messages.

What's the dress code?

Our palette is “all things bright and beautiful.” Please come formal, but in bright, joyful colours — Kente and other traditional wear are warmly encouraged. It's a celebration!

Where exactly is the ceremony?

The ceremony is held in the Elizabeth Chapel, on the Anagkazo campus. When you arrive, simply ask for the Elizabeth Chapel and you'll be directed straight to us.

How do I get there from Accra?

Search “Anagkazo Bible School” in Google Maps and it will take you right to the gate — it's in Mampong‑Akuapem, about 45 minutes to an hour from Accra through the Aburi hills.

Open directions in Google Maps →

Flying in? Kotoka International Airport (ACC) is your stop — plan to arrive by Friday, November 6.

Is there parking, and a gate fee?

Yes — there's a car park on site. Please note Anagkazo has a toll at the gate, so come prepared to pay GH₵20 per car on entry.

Can I change my RSVP later?

Yes — just fill the form again with the same email address and your answer updates automatically.

What time should I arrive?

Doors open at 8:30 am; please be seated by 9:00 am. The ceremony begins at 9:30 am sharp.
